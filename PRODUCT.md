# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Registry-S is used by small office teams, administrators, staff, and viewers who need to record, find, and verify office items quickly. Users work in a practical admin context, usually checking what exists, where it is, who is responsible, and whether records are current.

## Product Purpose

The product is a simple cloud item registry for office assets, consumables, and general equipment. It tracks item name, type, category, quantity, unit, asset or serial numbers, location, responsible person, status, notes, images, and audit history. Success means records are searchable, accurate, permission-controlled, and easy to maintain without becoming a full inventory or warehouse system.

## Brand Personality

Calm, official, precise. The interface should feel like a dependable office control surface: dense enough for repeated work, clear enough for non-technical staff, and restrained enough for administrative use.

## Anti-references

Do not make the product feel like a procurement, stock movement, borrowing, approval, or maintenance platform. Avoid marketing-style dashboards, oversized hero layouts, decorative cards, novelty interactions, or features copied from the prototype that are outside the approved scope.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the registry small and searchable before adding workflow complexity.
2. Prefer Explorer-style information density: navigation, filters, lists, and inspector panes should make scanning fast.
3. Preserve real Supabase data, role permissions, and existing server workflows while adapting the visual language.
4. Use Thai-first labels with clear operational wording.
5. Make empty, loading, and restricted states explicit so each role understands what they can do.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target practical WCAG AA basics: readable contrast, keyboard-reachable controls, visible focus states, responsive layouts for desktop and mobile, and reduced reliance on color alone for status.
